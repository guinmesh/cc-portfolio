project #8: experimental camera

link to code

To interact with this sketch, click "Allow," when your browser asks for permission to access the camera on your computer. You should be able to see yourself in the p5.js-generated sketch!

description

In this project, we were prompted to create a digital camera. I chose to create a MOSSCAM, a camera that takes a subject and helps them to be more complimentary to moss. My intentions with this were to (1) somewhat conceal and erase the identity of a figure by making them look "mossy," so to speak, and (2) call attention to something that gets overlooked all the time.

Throughout CC and the semester as a whole, my projects have focused on being mindful of the self, of natural elements, and of how those two things interact. I liked the simplicity of a MOSSCAM - it quickly transforms a figure into a bed of moss, and takes the responsibility of moving, eating, speaking, etc. away from the figure for a moment.

reflections

I did not especially enjoy doing this project because it required a camera throughout the coding process. I already don't love taking pictures of myself, and I found it unsettling to be continuously on camera. I was uncomfortable with the fact that an uninterrupted surveillance tool was exactly the product of this project. Even with the filters, it is still an image of me that's captured. I didn't want to confront the ease at which an image can be captured.
