project #2: face generator

link to code

To interact with me, mouse over the drawing, click your mouse, and tap keys on your keyboard.

description

Draw two faces â€“ the first drawing should depict the face at its maximum scale, and the second drawing should depict the face at its minimum scale. Once you have an idea of how your face would look like at its maximum / minimum scale, you should be able to use the range in between to create variations of the form.

technical components

  1. User-defined variables
  2. random()
  3. mousePressed() and/or keyPressed()

design process

This project was a great venue for communicating emotion through scale and for augmenting the connection between viewer and image through direct interaction. Starting out with those two intentions, I began sketching my two faces.

My initial inclination was to use the scale to communicate a sense of anger, then a sense of smallness. As I continued to develop my sketch, especially after playing around within the p5.js editor, I wanted the message of this sketch to be the enormity of the challenges facing humanity - more specifically, facing the youth of humanity - like climate change, racial inequity, and late stage capitalism. I wanted to comment on living this experience as a young person. Capture the pain, the complexity, and some of the tools to combat it.

I started with the world, a blue ocean background with some, but not all of the continents. I abstracted those elements to mirror the style of the generated face as well as to deemphasize the physical world in this sketch.

Next, I constructed the face itself, a relatively simple design. I wanted the emphasis to be on the eyes, as well as the colors, which I randomized. I felt that the changing colors communicated a sense of anxiety and unrest.

I randomized the height of the eye in order to practice p5.js, but it ended up being a happy accident. I liked that it made the eye look like it was blinking. It gave the sense of the passage of time, and it also communicated that the face was taking in a great deal of visual information from across the world.

final reflections

Young people are cynical about love. Ultimately, cynicism is the great mask of the disappointed and betrayed heart. - bell hooks

I did not want this sketch to come across as solely cynical. I wanted to communicate that there is still space for us to notice the small things. To do so, I included the option to press a key on the keyboard to both change the color and erase the noise from the sketch.

Additionally, the "click for days till redemption," and the randomly generated number have a double meaning. There is a cynical irony about that element, an apocalyptic proclamation, but there is also a hopefulness. Maybe redemption represents the day that we will save ourselves, when we will come together and create something that is built upon a common love, a common suffering, a common experience of humanity.

While talking to Yi Wei (@yiiiiwei), friend and poet, we spoke of the need for hope in art. All of the writers, artists, and performers we admired most were able to convey the entirety of the human experience. We were both especially interested in how great artists can capture moments of intense joy, even in the face of so much suffering. We decided that there is something to be said about the artist's responsibility to notice, to slow down, and to amplify joy.

Cynicism is necessary to acknowledge the challenges in front of us, but joy and hope enable us to actively overcome them.

My hope is that I will be able to use p5.js, as well as other visual tools, to amplify joy, center the issues that pain us, and create valuable, accessible commentary.
